GLOBAL FOOD SECURITY SITUATION REPORT (2026)
==============================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Global food security remains under severe stress in 2026, driven by
the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict, climate-induced crop failures,
and supply chain disruptions. The FAO Food Price Index averaged 142
points in Q1 2026. An estimated 345 million people face acute food
insecurity across 82 countries.

RUSSIA-UKRAINE CONFLICT IMPACT ON GRAIN EXPORTS
-------------------------------------------------
Pre-conflict (2021):
  - Ukraine: World's 5th largest wheat exporter, 4th largest corn exporter
  - Russia: World's largest wheat exporter
  - Combined: 30% of global wheat exports, 20% of corn exports

Current (2026):
  - Black Sea Grain Initiative: Collapsed in July 2023, partially restored
    via bilateral corridors in 2025
  - Ukraine wheat exports: 14 million tons (down from 18M pre-war)
  - Ukraine corn exports: 22 million tons (down from 27M pre-war)
  - Russia wheat exports: 48 million tons (record high, leveraging
    sanctions-evasion shipping networks)
  - Port infrastructure: Odesa port operating at 60% capacity
  - Mine contamination: 30% of Ukraine farmland remains inaccessible

CLIMATE CHANGE IMPACT ON RICE PRODUCTION (SOUTHEAST ASIA)
-----------------------------------------------------------
- El Niño 2025-2026 caused severe drought across Southeast Asia
- Thailand (2nd largest rice exporter): Production down 15% in 2025
  - Central Plains: water levels in key reservoirs at 40% capacity
  - Government restricted rice cultivation to one crop/year in some regions
- Vietnam (3rd largest rice exporter): Mekong Delta saltwater intrusion
  - 30% of rice paddies affected by salinization
  - Export ban on broken rice extended through 2026
- India (largest rice exporter): Imposed 20% export duty on non-basmati rice
  - Monsoon 2025 was 8% below normal
  - Buffer stock drawdown to maintain domestic supply
- Myanmar: Political instability disrupting farming operations
  - Estimated 25% reduction in rice production

STRATEGIC RESERVES
-------------------
- China: Estimated 200 million tons of grain reserves (world's largest)
  - 50-60% of global rice reserves, 35% of global wheat reserves
  - Strategic reserve information classified; estimates based on trade data
- United States: Strategic grain reserves largely depleted since 2008
  - Bill HR-4420 (2025) proposed rebuilding 60-day grain reserve (not passed)
  - Private sector inventory: 45-day supply of wheat
- India: 60 million tons public grain stock (FCI warehouses)
  - Targeting 70M tons by 2027
- EU: No centralized grain reserve; member state-level stocks vary

REGIONS AT HIGHEST FAMINE RISK (2026)
---------------------------------------
1. Horn of Africa (Ethiopia, Somalia, South Sudan, Kenya)
   - 25 million facing crisis-level food insecurity
   - Consecutive failed rain seasons (6th in a row for parts of Somalia)
   - Conflict in Ethiopia's Tigray region disrupting aid distribution

2. Sahel Region (Burkina Faso, Mali, Niger, Chad)
   - 12 million facing emergency food insecurity
   - Jihadist insurgency restricting humanitarian access
   - Pastoralist communities losing livestock to drought

3. Afghanistan
   - 23 million food insecure (55% of population)
   - International aid reduced by 40% after Taliban governance disputes
   - Wheat production down 20% due to drought

4. Yemen
   - 17 million food insecure despite partial ceasefire
   - Currency collapse exacerbating import costs
   - Port capacity at 50% (Hodeidah still constrained)

EMERGENCY MEASURES RECOMMENDED
---------------------------------
Short-term (0-6 months):
  1. Scale up WFP emergency food distributions in Horn of Africa and Sahel
  2. Release strategic reserves from China and India if humanitarian corridors secured
  3. Temporary export restriction moratorium among G20 nations
  4. Emergency seed distribution for next planting season

Medium-term (6-24 months):
  1. Invest in climate-resilient crop varieties (drought-tolerant rice, heat-resistant wheat)
  2. Expand irrigation infrastructure in Sub-Saharan Africa
  3. Rebuild US strategic grain reserve (60-day target)
  4. Diversify global grain trade routes to reduce Black Sea dependency

Long-term (2-10 years):
  1. Scale vertical farming and precision agriculture in food-insecure regions
  2. Reform WTO agricultural trade rules to prevent hoarding
  3. International food security treaty with binding reserve requirements
  4. Shift diets: incentivize plant-based protein to reduce grain-to-meat conversion losses
