CYBERSECURITY INCIDENT RESPONSE FRAMEWORK (2026)
==================================================

EXECUTIVE OVERVIEW
-------------------
This document provides a comprehensive framework for cybersecurity incident
response, based on NIST SP 800-61r3 and updated for the 2026 threat landscape.

INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION
-------------------------
Severity Level 1 (CRITICAL): Active data exfiltration, ransomware deployment,
  unauthorized access to crown jewel systems. Response time: 15 minutes.
Severity Level 2 (HIGH): Confirmed breach, lateral movement detected,
  credential compromise of privileged accounts. Response time: 1 hour.
Severity Level 3 (MEDIUM): Suspicious network activity, phishing success
  (non-privileged account), malware on isolated endpoint. Response time: 4 hours.
Severity Level 4 (LOW): Failed intrusion attempt, policy violation,
  vulnerability disclosure without active exploitation. Response time: 24 hours.

RESPONSE PHASES
----------------
Phase 1 — DETECTION & ANALYSIS (0-2 hours)
  - Activate SOC escalation procedures
  - Preserve volatile evidence (memory dumps, network captures)
  - Determine scope: affected systems, data types, geographic footprint
  - Classify severity level based on initial findings
  - Engage forensics team for evidence chain-of-custody

Phase 2 — CONTAINMENT (2-8 hours)
  - Short-term: Isolate affected systems (network segmentation)
  - Disable compromised accounts and rotate credentials
  - Implement emergency firewall rules and DNS sinkholes
  - Long-term: Deploy patches, update detection signatures
  - Document all containment actions with timestamps

Phase 3 — ERADICATION (8-48 hours)
  - Remove malware, rootkits, and persistence mechanisms
  - Close vulnerability exploited for initial access
  - Verify no remaining backdoors or C2 infrastructure
  - Re-image compromised systems from known-good backups

Phase 4 — RECOVERY (48 hours - 2 weeks)
  - Restore systems from verified clean backups
  - Monitor restored systems for re-compromise indicators
  - Gradually lift containment measures
  - Validate data integrity

Phase 5 — POST-INCIDENT (2-4 weeks)
  - Conduct lessons-learned review
  - Update incident response playbooks
  - Implement long-term security improvements
  - Submit regulatory reports (within required timeframes)

LEGAL DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------------
GDPR (EU): 72 hours to notify supervisory authority
SEC (US): "Material" breaches must be disclosed in 8-K filing within 4 business days
HIPAA (US healthcare): 60 days to notify affected individuals
PCI-DSS: Notify card brands within 24-72 hours
State breach notification laws: Varies by US state (typically 30-60 days)
China PIPL: Immediately notify authorities; individuals "in a timely manner"
Brazil LGPD: "Reasonable time" to notify ANPD

STOCK PRICE IMPACT ANALYSIS
-----------------------------
Historical data on major breaches:
- Equifax (2017): -35% in first month, recovered after 2 years
- SolarWinds (2020): -23% in first week, recovery in 6 months
- MOVEit/Progress Software (2023): -22% in 2 weeks, partial recovery
- Average short-term impact: -7% to -15% for publicly traded companies
- Companies with rapid, transparent disclosure recover 40% faster

PR CRISIS COMMUNICATION BEST PRACTICES
-----------------------------------------
1. Acknowledge the incident within 24 hours (do not wait for full investigation)
2. Provide regular updates (every 24-48 hours initially)
3. Be specific about what happened and what you're doing
4. Avoid legal jargon and blame-shifting
5. Offer concrete remediation to affected customers
6. CEO should be the face of the response (not just the CISO)
