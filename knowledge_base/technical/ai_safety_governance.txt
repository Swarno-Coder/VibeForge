AI SAFETY AND GOVERNANCE — GLOBAL LANDSCAPE (2026)
=====================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
As of 2026, AI governance has become one of the most critical policy areas
globally. The rapid advancement of frontier AI models (GPT-5, Gemini 3.x,
Claude 4, Llama-5) has intensified debates around safety, alignment, and
regulatory frameworks.

US AI REGULATION
-----------------
- Executive Order 14110 (Oct 2023): Requires safety testing for dual-use
  foundation models exceeding 10^26 FLOP training compute.
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework: Voluntary but widely adopted by industry.
- Proposed AI Accountability Act (2025): Mandates algorithmic auditing for
  high-risk AI systems in healthcare, finance, and criminal justice.
- FTC enforcement actions: 12 cases against deceptive AI practices in 2025.
- No comprehensive federal AI law as of Q1 2026; patchwork of state laws.
- California SB-1047 (vetoed 2024) successor bill SB-892 under debate.

EU AI ACT
----------
- Enforcement began in phases: August 2024 (prohibited practices),
  August 2025 (GPAI rules), August 2026 (full enforcement of Annex III).
- Risk-based classification:
  - Unacceptable risk (banned): Social scoring, real-time biometric ID in public
  - High risk: AI in healthcare diagnostics, credit scoring, recruitment
  - Limited risk: Chatbots (transparency requirements)
  - Minimal risk: Spam filters, etc. (no requirements)
- General-Purpose AI (GPAI) rules:
  - Transparency: Must disclose training data summaries
  - Systemic risk models (>10^25 FLOP): Mandatory red-teaming, incident reporting
- Penalties: Up to €35 million or 7% of global annual turnover
- AI Office established in Brussels: 120+ staff for enforcement

CHINA AI GOVERNANCE
--------------------
- Interim measures for Generative AI (Aug 2023): Content must align with
  "core socialist values"
- Deep Synthesis Regulations: Mandate labeling of AI-generated content
- Algorithm Registration: 40+ algorithms registered with CAC as of 2026
- Data security: Cross-border data transfer restrictions affect training data
- State Council AI Development Plan: $15B annual R&D investment target
- Approach: Industry-friendly regulation with strong content control

UK AI SAFETY
-------------
- AI Safety Institute (AISI): Established 2023, expanded to 200 researchers
- Pro-innovation approach: No separate AI law; sector-specific regulation
- Frontier AI Taskforce merged into AISI
- International AI Safety agreements: bilateral pacts with US, Japan, South Korea
- Focus: Pre-deployment safety testing of frontier models

INTERNATIONAL COORDINATION
----------------------------
- Bletchley Declaration (Nov 2023): 29 countries committed to AI safety
- Seoul AI Summit (May 2024): 16 companies signed safety commitments
- 2025 Paris AI Summit: Established Global AI Safety Network
- 2026 UN AI Advisory Body: Recommended international governance framework
- OECD AI Principles: Updated 2024, adopted by 46 countries
- G7 Hiroshima AI Process: Code of conduct for AI developers

OPEN-SOURCE AI DEBATE
-----------------------
Arguments FOR open-source model releases:
- Democratizes access to AI capabilities
- Enables independent safety research
- Prevents monopolistic control
- Accelerates innovation and reduces costs

Arguments AGAINST unrestricted open-source:
- Once released, cannot be recalled or patched
- Enables misuse (bioweapons, cyber attacks, CSAM)
- Difficult to enforce responsible use
- "Model collapse" concerns with proliferation of AI-generated training data

COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS FOR GLOBAL SAAS COMPANY
-------------------------------------------------
1. EU: AI Act registration, conformity assessment for high-risk systems
2. US: Sector-specific (HIPAA for health AI, Fair Housing Act for housing AI)
3. China: Algorithm registration, content moderation, data localization
4. Brazil: LGPD compliance, AI Bill (PL 2338/2023) requires impact assessments
5. Canada: AIDA (Artificial Intelligence and Data Act) passed 2025
6. India: Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023, no specific AI law yet

- HumanEval+: Code generation safety (focus on secure coding practices)
