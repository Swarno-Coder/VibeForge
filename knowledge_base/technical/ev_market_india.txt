ELECTRIC VEHICLE MARKET IN INDIA — COMPREHENSIVE ANALYSIS (2026)
=================================================================

MARKET OVERVIEW
---------------
India's electric vehicle market has experienced exponential growth, reaching
$7.5 billion in 2026 (up from $3.2 billion in 2024). The government's target
of 30% EV penetration by 2030 is driving aggressive policy and infrastructure
investment.

SALES FIGURES (2025-2026)
--------------------------
- Total EV sales FY2025-26: 2.8 million units
  - Two-wheelers: 2.1 million (75%)
  - Three-wheelers: 380,000 (13.6%)
  - Four-wheelers (passenger): 280,000 (10%)
  - Commercial vehicles: 40,000 (1.4%)
- Year-over-year growth: 48% overall
- EV penetration rate: ~8% of total vehicle sales (up from 5.2% in FY2024-25)

MAJOR MANUFACTURERS — MARKET SHARE (Four-wheelers)
----------------------------------------------------
1. Tata Motors: 62% — Nexon EV, Punch EV, Curvv EV
2. MG Motor (SAIC): 11% — ZS EV, Comet EV
3. Mahindra Electric: 10% — XUV400, BE 6
4. Hyundai: 8% — Creta EV, Ioniq 5
5. BYD India: 5% — Atto 3, Seal
6. Others (Citroen, BMW, Mercedes): 4%

GOVERNMENT INCENTIVES
----------------------
1. FAME III Scheme (2024-2027): ₹10,000 crore ($1.2B) subsidy allocation
   - Demand incentives: ₹15,000/kWh for electric two-wheelers
   - ₹10,000/kWh for electric four-wheelers (capped at 20% of vehicle cost)
2. PM E-DRIVE Scheme: ₹10,900 crore for EV adoption and charging infra
3. State-level incentives:
   - Delhi: Road tax waiver + ₹1.5 lakh purchase subsidy
   - Maharashtra: 15% price subsidy + registration fee waiver
   - Gujarat: ₹1.5 lakh subsidy for electric four-wheelers
   - Karnataka: 100% road tax exemption
4. Production-Linked Incentive (PLI) for batteries:
   - ₹18,100 crore for Advanced Chemistry Cell (ACC) manufacturing
   - Beneficiaries: Reliance New Energy, Ola Electric, Amara Raja

CHARGING INFRASTRUCTURE
-------------------------
- Public charging stations: ~12,000 (as of March 2026)
- Target: 46,000 stations by 2030
- Major operators: Tata Power (3,500+ stations), BPCL, Ather Grid
- Highway corridors: 2,500+ stations on national highways
- Challenge: 78% of stations are in top 10 cities; rural coverage is minimal

BATTERY MANUFACTURING
----------------------
- Domestic cell manufacturing at early stage
- Ola Gigafactory (Tamil Nadu): 20 GWh capacity, Phase 1 operational
- Reliance-Samsung SDI JV: 50 GWh announced, construction underway
- Current battery import dependency: ~85% (primarily from China, South Korea)
- Battery cost in India: $115/kWh (vs global average $100/kWh)

SWOT ANALYSIS FOR FOREIGN INVESTOR
------------------------------------
STRENGTHS:
- World's 3rd largest auto market (4.7M vehicles/year)
- Strong government policy support and subsidies
- Young, tech-savvy population (median age 28)
- Rapidly falling battery costs

WEAKNESSES:
- Charging infrastructure gaps (especially rural/semi-urban)
- High import dependency for batteries and components
- Grid reliability issues in several states
- Price-sensitive market (average car budget: ₹8-12 lakh)

OPPORTUNITIES:
- Two/three-wheeler segment is massive and underserved
- Fleet electrification (ride-hailing, logistics) growing rapidly
- Battery manufacturing PLI incentives for early movers
- Export hub potential for right-hand-drive EVs

THREATS:
- Chinese manufacturers (BYD, Great Wall) offering aggressive pricing
- Policy uncertainty post-2027 (FAME III expiry)
- Lithium supply constraints (India has limited reserves)
- Grid capacity challenges with mass EV adoption
