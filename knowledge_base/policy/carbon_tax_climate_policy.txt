CARBON TAX AND CLIMATE POLICY REFERENCE (2026)
================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Global carbon pricing mechanisms cover approximately 23% of total greenhouse
gas emissions as of 2026, up from 11% in 2020. The average carbon price
across covered jurisdictions is approximately $35/ton CO2, though prices
vary dramatically by region.

MAJOR CARBON PRICING SYSTEMS
------------------------------

1. EU Emissions Trading System (EU ETS)
   - Type: Cap-and-trade
   - Price: €85-95/ton CO2 (Q1 2026)
   - Coverage: Power generation, heavy industry, aviation (intra-EU), shipping
   - Recent: Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) transitional phase
     (reporting) since October 2023; financial obligations from January 2026
   - CBAM covers: Iron, steel, cement, aluminum, fertilizers, electricity, hydrogen

2. California Cap-and-Trade (linked with Quebec)
   - Price: $38-42/ton CO2 (Q1 2026)
   - Coverage: Facilities emitting >25,000 tons CO2/year
   - Sector: Power, industrial, transportation fuel suppliers

3. China National ETS
   - Type: Cap-and-trade (intensity-based)
   - Price: ¥95-110/ton CO2 (~$13-15) (Q1 2026)
   - Coverage: Power sector only (2,200+ entities)
   - Expanding to: Cement, aluminum (expected 2026-2027)

4. UK ETS
   - Type: Cap-and-trade (post-Brexit, separate from EU)
   - Price: £55-65/ton CO2 (Q1 2026)
   - Coverage: Similar to EU ETS sectors

5. Canada Federal Carbon Pricing
   - Type: Carbon tax + output-based pricing
   - Price: C$95/ton CO2 (2026, rising C$15/year)
   - Coverage: Economy-wide
   - Revenue: Returned as "Climate Action Incentive" payments to households

ECONOMIC IMPACT ON MANUFACTURING
-----------------------------------
Germany:
  - Manufacturing contributes 19% of GDP
  - Carbon costs add 3-8% to production costs for energy-intensive industries
  - Steel: €50-80/ton cost increase from EU ETS
  - Risk: Carbon leakage to non-carbon-priced jurisdictions
  - Mitigation: Free allocation for trade-exposed sectors being phased out

Japan:
  - Manufacturing contributes 20% of GDP
  - GX (Green Transformation) Strategy: ¥20 trillion ($135B) over 10 years
  - Carbon pricing: Voluntary carbon credit market, mandatory system under debate
  - Automotive impact: EV transition accelerated by implicit carbon costs

India:
  - Manufacturing contributes 17% of GDP
  - No carbon tax currently; Perform Achieve Trade (PAT) energy efficiency scheme
  - CBAM exposure: Indian steel/aluminum exporters to EU face new costs
  - Estimated CBAM cost to Indian industry: $1.5-2.5 billion/year

"JUST TRANSITION" PRINCIPLES
-------------------------------
Based on ILO Just Transition Guidelines and 2025 Paris Agreement updates:

1. Social dialogue: Workers and unions must be included in transition planning
2. Social protection: Unemployment benefits, retraining programs, early retirement
3. Skills development: Funded retraining from fossil fuels to green economy jobs
4. Local economic diversification: Investment in coal/oil-dependent communities
5. Green job creation: Target 1:1 ratio of lost fossil jobs to new green jobs
6. Revenue recycling: Carbon tax revenue returned to affected communities
7. Timeline: Phased transitions over 10-20 years to avoid economic shock
