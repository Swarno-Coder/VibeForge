GEOPOLITICAL RISK ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK
=======================================

PURPOSE
-------
This framework provides structured methodology for assessing geopolitical
risks affecting business operations, investment decisions, and strategic planning.

RISK ASSESSMENT DIMENSIONS
-----------------------------

1. POLITICAL STABILITY INDEX
   Measure: Government stability, policy predictability, regime change risk
   Data sources: Freedom House, Economist Intelligence Unit, World Bank WGI
   Scale: 1 (highly unstable) to 10 (highly stable)

   Current assessments (Q1 2026):
   - United States: 7.2 (polarization risk, but institutional resilience)
   - European Union: 7.8 (stable institutions, far-right political gains)
   - China: 6.5 (political stability, economic slowdown concerns)
   - India: 7.0 (strong economic growth, religious tension risk)
   - Russia: 4.5 (sanctions impact, prolonged conflict)
   - Middle East (aggregate): 4.2 (Iran-Israel tensions, Gulf stability)

2. ECONOMIC RISK FACTORS
   - Currency stability and convertibility
   - Inflation rate and central bank credibility
   - Trade balance and foreign reserve levels
   - Sovereign debt sustainability
   - Sanctions exposure (primary and secondary)

3. SECURITY RISK FACTORS
   - Active armed conflicts
   - Terrorism threat level
   - Cyber attack frequency and sophistication
   - Border disputes and territorial claims
   - Nuclear proliferation concerns

4. REGULATORY & LEGAL RISK
   - Rule of law strength
   - Intellectual property protection
   - Contract enforcement reliability
   - Ease of doing business ranking
   - Foreign investment restrictions

SCENARIO PLANNING METHODOLOGY
--------------------------------
For each geopolitical risk, develop three scenarios:

Base Case (60% probability):
  Current trends continue with incremental changes
  Planning horizon: 1-3 years

Upside Scenario (20% probability):
  Positive developments (peace agreements, trade deals, reforms)
  Planning horizon: 2-5 years

Downside Scenario (20% probability):
  Escalation (conflict, sanctions, regime change, economic crisis)
  Planning horizon: 1-2 years

CURRENT HOT SPOTS (2026)
---------------------------
1. Taiwan Strait: Elevated military posturing, no active conflict
   - Risk: Trade disruption, semiconductor supply chain
   - Trigger: Unilateral independence declaration or Chinese military exercises

2. Iran-Israel-US: Active tensions following 2024-2025 escalation
   - Risk: Oil supply disruption, regional instability
   - Trigger: Nuclear program milestones, proxy conflict escalation

3. Ukraine-Russia: Frozen conflict with periodic escalation
   - Risk: European energy security, grain supply, sanctions cascade
   - Trigger: Major territorial changes, NATO involvement escalation

4. South China Sea: Maritime territorial disputes (China, Philippines, Vietnam)
   - Risk: Trade route disruption ($5.3 trillion annual shipping)
   - Trigger: Artificial island militarization, fishing fleet incidents

5. Sahel Region: Military coups, Wagner/Africa Corps presence
   - Risk: Resource access (uranium, gold), migration flows
   - Trigger: Complete Western disengagement, jihadist resurgence

RISK REPORT OUTPUT TEMPLATE
------------------------------
When producing a geopolitical risk report, include:

1. SITUATION OVERVIEW
   - Current status and recent developments (48 hours)
   - Key actors and their stated positions
   - Timeline of escalation/de-escalation events

2. RISK MATRIX
   | Risk Factor | Probability | Impact | Risk Score |
   |-------------|------------|--------|------------|
   | [Factor]    | 1-5        | 1-5    | P × I      |

3. SCENARIO ANALYSIS (Base/Upside/Downside for each key risk)

4. BUSINESS IMPACT ASSESSMENT
   - Supply chain disruption (probability, duration, alternatives)
   - Market access impact (tariffs, sanctions, restrictions)
   - Financial impact (currency, cost of capital, asset values)
   - Operational risk (staff safety, facility security)

5. RECOMMENDED ACTIONS
   - Immediate (0-30 days): Monitoring triggers and escalation indicators
   - Short-term (1-6 months): Contingency plans and hedging strategies
   - Long-term (6-24 months): Strategic reorientation and diversification
