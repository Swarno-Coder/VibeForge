AI REGULATION COMPLIANCE REFERENCE — GLOBAL OVERVIEW (2026)
=============================================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
------------------
This document provides a compliance reference for technology companies
operating across multiple jurisdictions with AI-powered products or services.

EUROPEAN UNION — AI ACT
-------------------------
Effective: Phased enforcement August 2024 - August 2026
Scope: Any AI system placed on market or used in the EU

Classification:
  PROHIBITED (Article 5):
    - Social scoring systems
    - Real-time remote biometric identification in public (with narrow exceptions)
    - Emotion recognition in workplace/education
    - Predictive policing based on profiling
    - Scraping facial images from internet for databases

  HIGH RISK (Annex III):
    - Biometric identification (non-real-time)
    - Critical infrastructure management
    - Education: admissions, assessment
    - Employment: recruitment, promotion, termination decisions
    - Essential services: credit scoring, insurance pricing
    - Law enforcement: risk assessment tools
    - Immigration: visa application assessments
    - Justice: sentencing recommendation systems

  LIMITED RISK:
    - Chatbots: Must disclose AI interaction
    - Deepfakes: Must label content as AI-generated
    - Emotion recognition: Must inform subjects

  MINIMAL RISK:
    - Spam filters, AI-enhanced video games, etc.
    - No specific requirements

GPAI (General-Purpose AI) Models:
  All GPAI: Must provide model cards, training data summaries, copyright policy
  Systemic risk GPAI (>10^25 FLOP): Red-teaming, adversarial testing, incident reporting

Penalties:
  - Prohibited practices: €35M or 7% global turnover (whichever higher)
  - High risk violations: €15M or 3% global turnover
  - Incorrect information: €7.5M or 1% global turnover

UNITED STATES — EXECUTIVE ORDERS AND SECTOR RULES
----------------------------------------------------
No comprehensive federal AI law. Framework based on:

  Executive Order 14110 (October 2023):
    - Dual-use foundation models >10^26 FLOP must report to Commerce Dept
    - Red-team testing results required before deployment
    - Watermarking of AI-generated government content

  Sector-specific regulations:
    - Healthcare: FDA guidance on AI/ML-based Software as Medical Device (SaMD)
    - Finance: OCC, Federal Reserve guidance on AI model risk management
    - Housing: Fair Housing Act applies to AI-powered tenant screening
    - Employment: EEOC guidance on AI in hiring (disparate impact analysis)

  State laws:
    - Colorado AI Act (2024): Algorithmic discrimination protections
    - NYC Local Law 144: Bias audits for automated employment decisions
    - Illinois BIPA: Biometric data processing consent requirements
    - California SB-892 (under debate): Frontier model safety requirements

CHINA
------
  Interim Measures for Generative AI Services (August 2023):
    - Content must align with "core socialist values"
    - Training data must be legal and properly licensed
    - Mandatory AI-generated content labeling
    - User identity verification required

  Algorithm Recommendation Regulations (March 2022):
    - Algorithm registration with CAC required
    - Transparency in algorithmic recommendation logic
    - Opt-out mechanism for users

  Deep Synthesis Regulations (January 2023):
    - All AI-generated content must be labeled
    - Service providers must verify user identity
    - Real-name registration for deepfake creators

  Data Security:
    - Cross-border data transfer requires security assessment
    - Critical information infrastructure operators: data localization

BRAZIL — LGPD + AI BILL
-------------------------
  LGPD (General Data Protection Law):
    - Consent-based data processing
    - Right to explanation for automated decisions
    - ANPD (National Data Protection Authority) enforcement

  AI Bill (PL 2338/2023):
    - Risk-based classification (similar to EU AI Act)
    - Algorithmic impact assessment for high-risk systems
    - Human oversight requirements
    - Expected passage: 2026
